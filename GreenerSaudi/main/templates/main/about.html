Vision for a Sustainable Saudi Arabia

The Saudi Green Initiative, launched in 2021 under Vision 2030, brings together national environmental efforts into one unified framework. It aims to build a more sustainable future by reducing emissions, expanding green landscapes, and protecting natural ecosystems across the Kingdom.

What the Initiative Does

SGI coordinates over 85 national initiatives involving government, private sector, and environmental organizations. These projects focus on renewable energy expansion, land restoration, biodiversity protection, and environmental regulation.

It acts as a national umbrella to align all climate-related efforts under clear measurable goals.

Core National Targets


SGI is built around three measurable national targets:

Reduce carbon emissions 


Reduce carbon emissions by more than 278 million tons of CO2 equivalent annually by 2030

Protect Areas


Protect 30% of Saudi Arabia's land and sea areas by 2030

Planting


Plant over 600 million trees by 2030


These efforts help combat desertification and improve biodiversity.


Long-Term Net Zero Commitment


Saudi Arabia aims to reach net-zero emissions by 2060, supported by the Circular Carbon Economy model and large-scale clean energy transformation projects.


Investment and Scale


The first wave of SGI initiatives represents over SAR 700 billion in investments, supporting renewable energy, environmental infrastructure, and sustainability projects across the country.
